Intel: Sustainability Through the Ages

Explore Intel's journey from its founding in 1968 to its sustainability initiatives shaping the future today.

1968

Intel Founded

Robert Noyce and Gordon Moore establish Intel, beginning a new era of semiconductor innovation.

1971

First Microprocessor

Intel releases the 4004, the world's first commercial microprocessor.

1978

8086 Processor

Intel launches the 8086 processor, establishing the x86 architecture.

1985

386 Processor

Intel introduces 32-bit computing with the revolutionary 386 processor.

2006

Peak GHG Emissions

Intel begins major investments in renewable energy and sustainable manufacturing.

2020

RISE Strategy

Intel launches its Responsible, Inclusive, Sustainable, and Enabling growth strategy.

2022

Net-Zero Goal

Intel commits to achieving net-zero greenhouse gas emissions by 2040.

2023

99% Renewable Electricity

Intel reaches 99% renewable electricity use across global operations.

2024

Sustainability Summit

Intel hosts its first Sustainability Summit focused on future manufacturing solutions.
